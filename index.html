    • Overcoming Adversity

    Powering through and achieving in the face of disaster

    At the tender age of 5, I broke my leg and it has been a downhill spiral ever since!

    Academic and Professional Success

    Being a boss b@?!h with health problems

    It would be easy for me to sit on my butt all day and wallow with the alphabet of health conditions I have, however, that isn't me!

    My brain works.

    My body just doesn't co-operate. So...

    I adapt.

    Over the years I have been repeatedly told to "give up" or "have some time out". Each time I challenged this negativity and developed workarounds.

    During uni, I was able to attend lectures remotely and adjust my PhD project to enable me to work from the bathroom when I was stuck at home with a flare. I graduated with a First Class Honours at Undergrad and completed my PhD at the age of 24 - bossed it!

    Nowadays, I work from home so that I have constant access to a toilet and medication. The quality of my work doesn't suffer and I have been able to progress in my career in spite of living between home and the hospital.
